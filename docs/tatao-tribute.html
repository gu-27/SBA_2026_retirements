Stories That Define You

Moments your colleagues will carry with them

Badminton Nights

When it comes to scheduling grad classes, Ta-Tao can never teach on Wednesday night. It's badminton night at the community center. Badminton runs in his veins.

My fondest memories are lunches in the lounge when Ta-Tao offered to pick up Chinese takeout. What a wonderful community builder! I also loved running into Ta-Tao in a random airport when we were both returning from summer trips to Europe. The world is both big and small!

Community Builder
The Bridge Builder

In 2009, Ta-Tao used his contacts and influence to help me get connected with Chang Gung University in Taiwan. I was able to spend three amazing weeks there during my first sabbatical, teaching classes and collaborating on two papers. Ta-Tao worked tirelessly and enthusiastically to make it happen.

Ta-Tao helps ease my transition to Spokane — the beef noodle soup from his wife is excellent! Ta-Tao was also the first colleague I told I was pregnant. He had arranged for me to teach in Taiwan and I had to tell him I couldn't go. He was very sweet and supportive, of course.

The Welcomer
Life Coach

I remember when it first started getting cold during my first semester and I told Ta-Tao I was cold. He told me to take up skiing because it made the winters more fun. I appreciate your sound and fun advice. I'm going to miss you!
