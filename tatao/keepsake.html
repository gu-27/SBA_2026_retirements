Stories & Memories

When it comes to scheduling grad classes, Ta-Tao can never teach on Wednesday night. It's badminton night at the community center. Badminton runs in his veins.

Ta-Tao is a wonderful colleague and mentor to me. He often checks in to see how things are going and always has a great story to share. One that really sticks with me is how he bravely “protected” his wife from mice and successfully caught those naughty little troublemakers!:)

I will always appreciate Ta-Tao's smile and words of wisdom, especially when it came to food and travel.

There is no better advocate for travel than Ta-Tao! For the last several years, he always has the most amazing photos and gifts he brings back from his travels!

In 2009. Ta-Tao used his contacts and influence to help me get connected with Chang Gung University in Taiwan. I was able to spend three amazing weeks there during my first sabbatical teaching some classes and connecting with faculty and students. From this, I was able to collaborate with new friends on two papers. I recall Ta-Tao working tirelessly and enthusiastically to make this experience in Taiwan for me possible.

Ta-Tao loved badminton and pickleball, and I was always the spoiled youngster who had some other small plans for big nights.

His love for BBQ chicken wings.

Ta-Tao helps ease my transition to Spokane - the beef noodle soup from his wife is excellent!

My fondest memories are lunches in the lounge when Ta-Tao offered to pick up Chinese takeout. What a wonderful community builder! I loved sharing food and conversation with him and other Jepson characters like Hickman and Shrader.

I also loved running into Ta-Tao in a random airport when we were both returning from summer trips to Europe. The world is both big and small!

Also, Ta-Tao was the first colleague I told that I was pregnant with Marie. He had arranged for me to teach in Taiwan, and I had to tell him that I wouldn't be able to go after all. He was very sweet and supportive about it, of course.

Ta-tao, your dedication to learning new technologies has been truly inspiring. I will miss having such a wonderful colleague to share learning experiences with.

Badminton, Chinese lunch delivery from Hong Kong Express which he organized for a while and was so awesome, and learning Power BI.

You discouraged me from moving to Spokane! You were so honest, even though I was a job candidate. I still remeber that! haha

Ta Tao’s dedication to continuous learning and his passion for incorporating new technologies into his teaching have left a lasting impact on both students and colleagues.

You gave me a start as an adjunct and when I inquired about becoming a full-time professor, you were very direct with me about the need to get my Ph.D. It was the clearest feedback I received and you didn't flinch about the commitment. Without this direct feedback, I would have taken much longer to understand what I needed to do in order to achieve my goals.
